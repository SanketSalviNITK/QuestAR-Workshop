# Teaching Notes: Session 4 - The Creator's Lab 🎨
**Duration**: 120 Minutes

## 1. The Creative Spark (10 Mins)
*   **The Message**: "You have the tools. You have the code. Now, you have the power to create something that never existed before."
*   **Examples**:
    *   An AR business card for their new portfolio.
    *   A 3D solar system appearing on a textbook.
    *   A scavenger hunt around the classroom.
*   **Group Formation**: Suggest 2-3 people per group to speed up brainstorming.

## 2. Planning Your Remix (15 Mins)
*   **The "Three Changes" Rule**: To make it their own, suggest they change at least 3 things:
    1.  The **Marker** (Their own image).
    2.  The **3D Object** (A different shape or color).
    3.  The **Message** (What does the hint box say?).
*   **Teaching Tip**: Walk around and validate their ideas. Ensure they aren't trying to build something *too* complex for 60 minutes.

## 3. The Building Phase (60 Mins)
*   **Instructor's Role**: You are now a consultant. Move between groups.
*   **Key Technical Help**:
    *   **Custom Markers**: Demonstrate the [Mind.AR Compiler](https://hiukim.github.io/mind-ar-js-doc/tools/compile/) on the big screen once for everyone.
    *   **Finding Models**: Show them how to find basic 3D geometry codes in the Three.js documentation.
    *   **Bug Squashing**: Most bugs will be path errors (`../assets/my-marker.mind`).
*   **Speaker Note**: "Don't be afraid to break the code. That's how we learn. If it breaks, we fix it together."

## 4. The Launch Party & Presentations (25 Mins)
*   **Format**: Each group gets 2 minutes to show their AR project on a smartphone.
*   **The "Walk-Around"**: If the class is large, have everyone put their markers on their desks and let students walk around and "hunt" each other's treasures.
*   **Feedback**: Focus on the **creativity** and the **hosting**. Ask: "Is it live? Can I see it on my phone?"

## 5. Course Wrap-up & Next Steps (10 Mins)
*   **Where to go next?**:
    *   **Three.js Journey**: The best place for advanced 3D.
    *   **Mind.AR Documentation**: For more complex tracking.
    *   **Vercel/GitHub**: Keep using these for every project!
*   **Final Words**: "You started the day with an idea. You're leaving with a live, hosted Augmented Reality application. You are now officially developers. Welcome to the club."

---
**Instructor's Checklist**:
- [ ] Has every group managed to host *something*?
- [ ] Did everyone take a screenshot of their final AR project?
- [ ] Are all students clear on how to update their code in the future?
